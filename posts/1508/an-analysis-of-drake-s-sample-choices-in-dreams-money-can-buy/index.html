Can money buy you dreams? That’s the thought provoking question that Drake tackles in his song ‘Dreams Money Can Buy’; and as we take a deeper look at his sample choices, it’s clear that the answer is a resounding no! Themes of ambition, materialism and melancholy all come alive in Drake’s ‘Dreams Money Can Buy’. His sample choices for this song are masterful and brilliantly evoke the myriad of emotions he wants to convey. From the light, whimsical opening of Laurie Spiegel’s ‘Appalachian Grove’ to the melancholic yet hopeful tones of Gil-Scott Heron’s ‘Home Is Where The Hatred Is’, the listener is taken on a journey of longing and realization. Ultimately this song serves as a reminder that our dreams often come with a cost. By delving into the examples of his sample choices, we can gain a better understanding of this complex concept.

The opening sample from ‘Appalachian Grove’ conveys a sense of wonder and adventure. This is juxtaposed against the harsher tones of the latter ‘Home Is Where The Hatred Is’ sample, which paints a picture of a world of materialism and exploitation. Drake’s choice to use these samples in such a way serves to emphasize his message of seeking our dreams but being aware of the cost that can be incurred in the process. The idealistic dream of his youth is gradually replaced with the hard reality of the cost of achieving success.

Drake’s sample choices in ‘Dreams Money Can Buy’ are ingenious, and provide the listener with a unique perspective on the nature of ambition. By pairing samples of such different tones, Drake’s message of the pursuit of dreams and the accompanying cost is made all the more impactful. Through his sample choices, Drake is forcing us to consider the potential consequences of our dreams becoming reality.
